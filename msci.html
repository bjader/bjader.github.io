MSci Project

I am currently teaching myself to code in C++ as part of my masters project, entitled "Entanglement Entropy of Fermions". Fermions are a type of particle (e.g. an electron) and I am developing an algorithm to calculate the properties of two groups of fermions that exhibit quantum entanglement.

So far I have created a first implementation of the algorithm, which is built around a Markov chain Monte Carlo method. I am in the process of now testing the algorithm on a simple system, comparing it against the mathematical framework developed from traditional calculations. The pictures below show various expected scaling patterns for the entanglement entropy.

Once the algorithm is tested to work correctly, I am hoping to apply it to significantly more complex systems that cannot be solved analytically using traditional maths. Given that entanglement entropy has never been solved for such advanced systems before, I am hoping to be able to publish my results at the end of my project (~ May 2017).
